## Postmortem — why it failed and what we learned

**This is the expected outcome, not a surprise.** The Moskowitz/Pedersen paper got Sharpe 1.4 across 58 diversified markets. Single-asset Sharpe in the literature is 0.3–0.6. On a 3-symbol Indian large-cap basket, getting a *negative* Sharpe on a 6-month OOS window is well inside the noise distribution.

### Signature in the results
- **Hit rate 16.7% with 5.9-bar avg holding** — classic "tight stop chops out, then trend continues without us" pattern. The 2σ stop is too tight for daily TSMOM.
- **Exposure ~45% on IS, ~20% on OOS** — IS had more trending stretches; OOS happened to be choppier. Sample size of 3 symbols × 5 months has no statistical power.
- **Costs are ~25% of gross losses** — non-trivial drag at ₹5K capital but not the killer. Killer is the signal being noisy on this universe.

### What we will NOT do
- **Re-tune the parameters on this data.** That's the cardinal sin. If we lengthen the lookback to 60 days because 20 lost money, we are fitting noise.
- **Add filters until something "works" in-sample.** Same problem.
- **Trade it live anyway because "TSMOM is well-known."** Well-known ≠ works on this universe at this size.

### What we WILL do
1. **Mark TSMOM as KILLED for this universe.** Keep the code (`titan/strategies/tsmom.py`) — the implementation is correct, it just needs a different universe to show edge.
2. **Move to the next priority strategy** — pairs trading or cross-sectional momentum on NIFTY-50, both of which need the universe diversification TSMOM lacked. Pairs is what our internal research doc flagged at Sharpe 1.34 in India.
3. **Reuse what we built**: `titan/data/backfill.py`, `titan/backtest/engine.py`, and the predeclared-threshold pattern in `scripts/run_tsmom_backtest.py` all stand. Those took the bulk of the work and will be used by every future strategy test.

### One thing worth a follow-up (later, not now)
On a 50-stock NSE-100 long-only TSMOM portfolio with daily rebalance and equal-vol weighting, the literature shows 0.6–0.9 Sharpe net. **That would need NSE-100 historical universe data**, which we'd build via the same `backfill.py` extended to a larger symbol list. Out of scope for tonight, on the roadmap for a future weekend.

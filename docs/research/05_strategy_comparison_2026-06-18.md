## Honest read

- **ORB**: low-DD modest loser. Decent hit rate, but profit factor < 1 after costs (₹3,038 in costs on ₹3,221 loss — i.e. without costs the strategy would be roughly flat, but trading costs eat all the edge).
- **vwap_revert**: disastrous. -₹46,157 = 92% of starting capital lost in OOS. ₹22,140 in costs alone. Mean reversion in trending NSE intraday bleeds. 508 trades = high churn paying the spread repeatedly.
- **supertrend_adx**: 18 trades is below the sample-size threshold for any real claim. PF 1.73 looks good only because a few large wins; Sharpe 0.01 means returns are statistically noise. Cannot conclude edge — only that there's no evidence of edge.

## Open questions for the next research cycle

1. Why does vwap_revert lose so badly in NSE intraday? Likely thesis: NSE has strong intraday trends after the open; mean reversion to VWAP is a fade against the prevailing tape. Needs regime-conditional gating before being retested.
2. ORB's profit-factor problem looks like a costs issue. Worth backtesting with tighter cost assumptions, or a lower-frequency variant (1×/day per symbol, more selective trigger), to see if it clears the bar.
3. supertrend_adx's 18 trades suggest the ADX gate is filtering too aggressively. Either loosen the filter and re-backtest (risking overfit) or accept the strategy is too rare to be capital-efficient.

None of these are commit-now actions. They are research questions for separate write-ups under `docs/research/`.
